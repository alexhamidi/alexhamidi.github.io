Andy Grove's framework for identifying and navigating strategic inflection points - moments where a 10x change in some force fundamentally alters the competitive landscape of an industry. He uses Intel's pivot from memory chips to microprocessors as the central case study.

The most valuable insight is about how these inflection points are obvious in retrospect but extremely difficult to recognize while you're in them. Grove describes the "valley of death" period where the old strategy is clearly failing but the new direction isn't yet validated, and how middle management often senses the shift before senior leadership does.

His description of the specific meetings and debates inside Intel during the memory-to-processor transition is fascinating - particularly the moment where he asked Gordon Moore "if we got kicked out and the board brought in a new CEO, what do you think he would do?" and they both knew the answer. Practical and grounded in real decisions rather than abstract theory.
