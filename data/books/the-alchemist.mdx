Santiago, a shepherd boy, travels from Spain to the Egyptian pyramids in pursuit of a recurring dream about finding treasure. Along the way he meets a king, an Englishman studying alchemy, and an alchemist who teaches him about the Soul of the World and listening to his heart.

The core idea - that the universe conspires to help those who pursue their Personal Legend - is a nice framing for thinking about purpose and commitment. The concept that the journey matters more than the destination, and that the treasure was ultimately back where Santiago started, lands well.

It's a simple book with a simple message, delivered through a parable format that makes it a quick read. Some of the spiritual language feels vague and repetitive, but the directness of the storytelling keeps it grounded.
