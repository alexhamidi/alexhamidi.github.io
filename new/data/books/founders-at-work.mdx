A collection of interviews with founders of major technology companies — Apple, Gmail, Flickr, PayPal, Hotmail, TiVo, and others — about the early days of their startups. Livingston asks practical questions about how they got started, what went wrong, and what they'd do differently.

The best interviews are the ones where founders describe the chaotic reality of building something from nothing. Steve Wozniak on designing the Apple I, Paul Graham on Viaweb's early sales process, and Max Levchin on PayPal's fraud problems are standouts. The recurring theme is that almost nobody had a clean path — most successful companies pivoted significantly from their original idea.

The interview format means some conversations are more insightful than others. A few founders give generic advice, but most share specific, unglamorous details about early-stage decisions that you don't find in polished retrospectives. Good for pattern-matching across many different startup origin stories.
