The greatest revenge story ever written. Edmond Dantès is wrongfully imprisoned, escapes after fourteen years, discovers a massive fortune, and systematically dismantles the lives of the three men who conspired against him.

What makes this book extraordinary is the patience of the revenge. Dantès spends years positioning himself, manipulating finances, exploiting secrets, and engineering situations where his enemies destroy themselves. The Villefort arc in particular is masterful - watching a man's buried sins surface through the Count's orchestration is incredibly satisfying.

Dumas also doesn't shy away from the cost of revenge. The collateral damage to innocent people like Valentine and Maximilian, and the Count's eventual reckoning with whether his mission was worth it, give the story a depth that most revenge narratives lack. At 1200+ pages it never drags.
